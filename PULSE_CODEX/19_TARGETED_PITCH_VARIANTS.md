# PULSE — Targeted Pitch Variants

This document defines how the same PULSE system is presented differently
depending on the evaluation context, without changing the underlying system.

---

## A) Competition Pitch (Innovation / Awards)

### Primary Focus
- Novelty
- Impact
- Clarity
- Visual and conceptual elegance

### Emphasize
- Early detection advantage
- Practical innovation using existing data
- Ethical-by-design architecture
- Demo clarity and explainability

### De-emphasize
- Deep statistical proofs
- Infrastructure complexity

### One-liner
“PULSE transforms routine lab data into early public-health awareness—before diagnoses accumulate.”

---

## B) Master’s Scholarship Pitch (Academic)

### Primary Focus
- Research depth
- Methodology
- Learning outcomes
- Academic contribution

### Emphasize
- Statistical foundations (EWMA, CUSUM, Farrington)
- Evaluation metrics (timeliness, sensitivity, stability)
- Research questions and thesis potential
- Governance and ethics

### De-emphasize
- Market language
- Commercial framing

### One-liner
“PULSE is a digital health research platform exploring explainable early-warning signals from population laboratory data.”

---

## C) Market / Pilot Pitch (Institutional)

### Primary Focus
- Feasibility
- Risk control
- Integration
- Governance

### Emphasize
- Non-intrusive overlay design
- Compatibility with existing LIS/EHR
- Governance, auditability, and trust indicators
- Incremental pilot deployment

### De-emphasize
- Experimental language
- Academic abstraction

### One-liner
“PULSE provides institutions with an ethical, explainable early-warning layer built on data they already collect.”

---

## Golden Rule
The system does NOT change.
Only the narrative emphasis changes.
